The Capstone Challenge: The "Smart Studio Lamp"
===============================================

The Scenario: You have been hired to prototype a control panel for a photographer's studio
light. The photographer needs to turn the light on and off with a single button press. When the
light is on, they need a dial to precisely adjust the brightness. They also want a digital readout of
the light's current power level on their computer.

Hardware Required:
	1x Push Button (with 10kΩ pull-down resistor)
	1x Potentiometer
	1x LED (with appropriate current-limiting resistor)
	Jumper wires and Breadboard
	
The Functional Requirements (The Rules):

1.​ The Master Toggle: The push button must act as a master ON/OFF switch. Pressing it
once turns the system ON. Pressing it again turns the system OFF. (Requires state
change logic and debouncing from Week 2).

2.​ Adjustable Brightness: When the system is ON, the LED's brightness must be
controlled in real-time by turning the potentiometer. (Requires Analog Reading and PWM
from Week 3).

3.​ True Zero: When the system is OFF, the LED must be completely dark, regardless of
where the potentiometer dial is currently turned.

4.​ Smart Serial Telemetry: The Serial Monitor must display the status of the lamp.
○​ If the lamp is OFF, it should print: System OFF.
○​ If the lamp is ON, it should print the brightness level.
○​ The Catch: Do not spam the Serial Monitor! The Arduino should only print a new
line when the button state changes OR when the potentiometer dial is actually
moved.
