The Capstone Challenge Part II: The "Industrial Pressure Monitor"
===================================================================

The Scenario: You are designing a safety monitoring system for a high-pressure steam valve.
The potentiometer represents a pressure sensor. The LED is the warning beacon on the
operator's dashboard, and the button is the "Acknowledge Alarm" switch.
The Functional Requirements (The Rules):


1.​ Zone 1: The Safe Zone (Analog reading 0 – 511)
○​ The pressure is normal. The LED must be completely OFF.

2.​ Zone 2: The Warning Zone (Analog reading 512 – 900)
○​ The pressure is rising. The LED must turn ON, and its brightness must scale with
the dial.
○​ The Catch: At an analog reading of 512, the LED should be barely on (PWM ~1).
At a reading of 900, it should be at maximum brightness (PWM 255).

3.​ Zone 3: The Critical Zone (Analog reading 901 – 1023)
○​ The pressure is dangerously high. The LED must detach from the PWM
brightness control and instead blink continuously.

4.​ The "Acknowledge" Override
○​ If the system is in the Critical Zone (blinking), the operator can press the push
button to acknowledge the danger.
○​ Once pressed, the LED stops blinking and stays solid ON at maximum
brightness.
○​ If the dial is turned back down below 901, the system resets to normal behavior,
and the override is forgotten.

5.​ Serial Monitor Output
○​ Continuously print the system status using a clear format. Example: Status:
WARNING | Sensor: 750 | PWM: 154
